1. Project Overview
CricPredict is a full-stack, data-driven cricket match prediction platform built primarily for the Indian audience. It predicts match outcomes between Team A and Team B by analysing historical head-to-head records, current squad form, individual player statistics, pitch conditions, and contextual factors like toss results and venue history.
The platform covers all major formats and competitions:
The core value proposition is realism — predictions backed by quantifiable data, weighted recency, and transparent confidence scores rather than guesswork.

4. Prediction Engine
The prediction engine is the core intelligence of CricPredict. It uses a multi-factor weighted scoring model, with an optional Machine Learning layer for improved accuracy over time.
4.1 Phase 1 — Weighted Scoring Model (Launch)
A transparent, explainable model. Each factor is scored and weighted to produce a win probability.
4.2 Phase 2 — Machine Learning Layer (Post-launch)
Model: Logistic Regression → Random Forest → XGBoost (iterative improvement)
Training data: 50,000+ historical matches from Kaggle + scraped data
Features: 40+ engineered features including form, H2H, pitch, conditions
Validation: 80/20 train-test split, tested on 2023–2025 unseen matches
Re-training: Monthly automated retraining pipeline with new match data

Realism Principle
• No model predicts cricket with >70% accuracy reliably — variance is inherent to the sport.
• CricPredict shows confidence levels (High / Medium / Low) alongside every prediction.
• Predictions include key reasons: e.g., 'India favoured: 7/10 H2H wins, Kohli averaging 68 in last 5'.
• Users can see every contributing factor — full transparency, no black-box outputs.

Final Note — What Makes This Realistic
• Data-first: Every prediction is traceable to specific stats — no vague AI confidence.
• Transparency: Show users WHY a team is favoured, not just who is favoured.
• Honest confidence: Always show Low / Medium / High confidence — cricket is unpredictable.
• IPL-first for India: IPL hub is the headline feature, international is the depth.
• Recency weighting: Recent form matters more than 3-year-old data — the model reflects this.
• Community validation: Leaderboard lets the community validate prediction quality over time.
